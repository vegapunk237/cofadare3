Prevention and Suppression Phase

United against drugs, building a healthier future

Understanding Our Approach

Our comprehensive strategy combines prevention and suppression to create a drug-free society.

Preventive Phase

Focuses on preventing substance use and addiction before it starts. We work proactively to educate, inform, and empower communities to make healthy choices and resist the temptation of drug abuse.

Suppressive Phase

Focuses on reducing or eliminating substance use in individuals who have already developed an addiction. Through targeted interventions and support systems, we help individuals break free from the cycle of addiction.

Zones of Action

We identify and categorize our intervention zones to provide targeted and effective support based on risk levels.

WHITE ZONE

Target Group: People untouched and unaffected by drug addiction

This zone encompasses individuals who have not been exposed to drug addiction. Our primary goal is to maintain their drug-free status through continuous education, awareness programs, and building strong community support systems that reinforce healthy lifestyle choices.

BLUE ZONE

Target Group: People vulnerable to being affected by drug addiction

The blue zone identifies individuals at risk of drug addiction due to various factors such as social environment, peer pressure, or personal circumstances. We provide intensive prevention programs, early intervention strategies, and support networks to protect these vulnerable populations.

Our Action Strategies

Comprehensive interventions designed to prevent drug abuse and support those in need.

Education Based on Drugs

Comprehensive educational programs in schools, universities, and communities that provide accurate information about the dangers of drug abuse, its effects on health, and the social consequences. We equip young people with knowledge to make informed decisions.

Sensitization Campaign

Active awareness campaigns through media, public events, and community outreach programs. We use various platforms to reach diverse audiences and create public consciousness about drug abuse prevention and the importance of collective action.

Community Involvement

Engaging local communities, families, religious organizations, and civic groups in the fight against drug abuse. We build strong networks of support that create protective environments and promote collective responsibility for youth welfare.

Healthy Alternatives

Promoting positive activities such as sports, arts, cultural programs, and vocational training as alternatives to drug use. We provide young people with constructive outlets for their energy and creativity, building self-esteem and life skills.

Early Intervention

Identifying and addressing warning signs of potential drug use at the earliest stages. Our trained professionals work with families, schools, and communities to provide timely support and prevent the progression from experimentation to addiction.

Support System

Building comprehensive support networks that include peer support, mentorship programs, counseling services, and family therapy. We strengthen the social fabric around individuals to provide ongoing encouragement and assistance in maintaining drug-free lifestyles.

Building Strong Support Networks

Our support system framework focuses on three critical pillars for sustainable prevention and recovery.

Building Strong Relationships

Developing meaningful connections and support networks within families, schools, and communities

Fostering trust and open communication channels

Creating safe spaces for dialogue and mutual support

Promoting Positive Activities

Encouraging engagement in sports, arts, and constructive hobbies

Developing talents and building self-confidence

Creating opportunities for personal growth and achievement

Addressing Underlying Issues

Identifying and treating mental health challenges and trauma

Addressing socio-economic factors that increase vulnerability

Providing comprehensive support for holistic well-being
