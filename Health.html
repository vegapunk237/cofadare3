Healthcare Systems Against Drug Abuse

COFADARE works with multiple healthcare systems to ensure comprehensive coverage and accessible treatment for individuals affected by drug abuse. Our multi-system approach ensures that no one is left behind in the fight against addiction.

National Health Service (NHS)

A publicly-funded healthcare system where services are provided to all citizens, often through a network of hospitals, clinics, and community health services. This system ensures universal access to drug abuse prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation services regardless of socio-economic status.

  • Universal coverage for all citizens
  • Network of hospitals and clinics
  • Community-based health services
  • Free or subsidized treatment programs

Social Health Insurance (SHI)

A system where individuals and/or employers contribute to a health insurance fund, which pays for healthcare services. This collaborative funding model ensures sustainable financing for comprehensive drug abuse prevention and treatment programs.

  • Employer and employee contributions
  • Pooled insurance fund
  • Coverage for treatment services
  • Risk-sharing mechanism

Out-of-Pocket (OOP) System

A system where individuals pay directly for healthcare services without any form of insurance or reimbursement. While this system provides immediate access to care, COFADARE works to minimize financial barriers for those seeking drug abuse treatment through subsidized programs and financial assistance.

  • Direct payment for services
  • Immediate access to treatment
  • COFADARE financial assistance programs
  • Sliding scale fee structures

Private Health Insurance

Insurance plans purchased by individuals or provided by employers that cover a range of healthcare services including substance abuse treatment. This system offers additional options for comprehensive care and specialized treatment programs for drug addiction.

  • Individual or employer-sponsored plans
  • Comprehensive coverage options
  • Access to specialized treatment centers
  • Extended rehabilitation programs

Community-Based Health Insurance

A system where community members pool their resources to cover healthcare costs. This grassroots approach represents the power of collective action in the fight against drug abuse. Communities come together to fund prevention programs, treatment services, and care for vulnerable cases, ensuring that everyone has access to the help they need.

  • Community-pooled resources
  • Group operation of funding
  • Local ownership and management
  • Focus on vulnerable populations
  • Culturally appropriate care
  • Community solidarity and support
